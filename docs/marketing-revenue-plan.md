# FixMyHome.pro Marketing and Revenue Plan

## Positioning

FixMyHome.pro is a free Florida home-repair marketplace where homeowners post clear projects and local handymen compete with transparent bids. Revenue should come from businesses that benefit from the marketplace, not from reducing trust or charging users simply to participate.

## Revenue Priorities

### 1. Founding local sponsors

Sell clearly labeled sponsorships to complementary local businesses: hardware stores, building suppliers, dumpster rentals, home inspectors, real-estate agents, property managers, insurers, and lenders.

- County or service-category sponsor: $149-$299 per month during the pilot.
- Newsletter or repair-guide sponsor: $75-$150 per placement.
- Keep sponsored placements visually distinct from organic results.
- Never sell reviews, ratings, or undisclosed ranking advantages.

### 2. Optional handyman business services

Keep marketplace access free while offering services that help handymen grow outside the marketplace.

- Website setup: $499-$1,500, plus optional $49-$149 monthly maintenance.
- Profile launch package: $99-$299 for copy, photos, service descriptions, and setup help.
- Google Business Profile and local-marketing assistance priced as a separate service.
- Verification assistance may cover document review, but payment must never guarantee approval.

### 3. Sponsored visibility

After the marketplace has useful traffic, offer labeled sponsor listings for $49-$99 per month. Sponsored placement must be separate from organic sorting and must not imply a better rating or endorsement.

### 4. Contextual advertising

Apply for Google AdSense only after FixMyHome.pro has meaningful traffic and a library of useful, original repair and local-service content. Start with limited placements on public guide and browse pages, not inside bidding, messaging, account, or safety-sensitive workflows.

Google requires original content and compliance with its publisher policies. FixMyHome.pro must not encourage ad clicks, generate artificial impressions, disguise ads as navigation, or place ads in emails or pop-ups.

### 5. Affiliate partnerships

Later, add relevant affiliate links for tools, materials, or homeowner products. Every material relationship must be clearly disclosed near the recommendation.

## 90-Day Execution

### Days 1-30: Prove local usefulness

- Instrument the funnel: verified signup, completed profile, job posted, first bid, accepted bid, and repeat visit.
- Recruit 25 credible handymen in one initial Florida market.
- Publish 10 original repair-cost, project-preparation, and hiring-safety guides.
- Build a one-page sponsor media kit with audience, placement examples, and founding rates.
- Contact 50 relevant local businesses and seek 3 pilot sponsors.
- Establish review moderation and advertising-disclosure rules before selling placements.

### Days 31-60: Build repeatable acquisition

- Launch useful city and service pages based on real marketplace coverage, not thin duplicated SEO pages.
- Add homeowner and handyman referral invitations.
- Partner with neighborhood groups, property managers, real-estate professionals, and local trade communities.
- Publish two useful local articles or project guides each week.
- Turn sponsor results into a simple monthly report: impressions, clicks, inquiries, and renewals.

### Days 61-90: Monetize carefully and expand

- Renew successful founding sponsors and refine pricing from measured demand.
- Launch the handyman website/profile service with three clear packages.
- Apply for AdSense only when public content and traffic meet quality requirements.
- Expand one county at a time after the first market has sufficient handyman coverage and bid activity.
- Create a quarterly trust report covering verification, moderation, complaints, and account actions.

## Core Metrics

- Email verification completion rate
- Profile completion rate
- Jobs posted per week
- Median bids per job
- Time to first bid
- Bid acceptance rate
- Homeowner and handyman 30-day retention
- Transactional email delivery and failure rate
- Cost per activated user
- Sponsor monthly recurring revenue and renewal rate
- Revenue per 1,000 public-page sessions
- Support, fraud, and safety incident rate

## Trust and Compliance Rules

- Label paid placements as **Sponsored** or **Advertisement**.
- Do not publish invented reviews or reward only positive reviews.
- Disclose affiliate and sponsor relationships clearly and close to the claim or link.
- Do not sell sensitive account, message, bid, or location data.
- Respect email opt-outs and reserve transactional messages for account and marketplace activity.
- Review advertising, privacy, tax, and contractor-related obligations with qualified Florida legal and accounting professionals before scaling.
